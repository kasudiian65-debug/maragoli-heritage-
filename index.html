About the Maragoli People

The Maragoli (also known as Logoli or Avalogooli) are one of the main sub-groups of the Luhya community of Western Kenya. They are known for their deep cultural values, agricultural traditions, and strong sense of unity. The Maragoli celebrate their heritage through dances, songs, and storytelling that have been passed down for generations.

Maragoli Sub-Tribes

The Maragoli community is made up of several clans and sub-tribes, each with its unique roots and identity.

  • Abasita – Found mainly in Chavakali area.
  • Abamahalia – Known for their craftsmanship and located near Majengo.
  • Abamavi – Primarily farmers and cattle herders.
  • Abasiekwe – Dwellers near Lugaga Hills.
  • Abasiru – Spread across Mbale and Gisambai.
  • Abamasingila – Also called Vamasingila, believed to have origins near Tiriki border, known for unity and community work.
  • Abasaniaga – Early settlers near Kegondi region.
  • Abawaya – Found in Mungoma and Kaimosi areas.
  • Abamulembo – Reside in the central Maragoli region.
